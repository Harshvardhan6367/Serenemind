Lotus Pose (Padmasana)

The classic meditation posture that promotes stability and deep focus, helping to calm the mind and prepare for meditation.

How to Perform:
  1. Sit on the floor with legs extended straight in front of you
  2. Bend your right knee and place your right foot on your left thigh, sole facing upward
  3. Bend your left knee and place your left foot on your right thigh, sole facing upward
  1. Keep your spine straight and lengthen through the crown of your head
  2. Rest your hands on your knees in Gyan Mudra (thumb and index finger touching)
  3. Close your eyes and focus on deep, rhythmic breathing
  4. Hold the pose for 5-30 minutes, maintaining stillness

Benefits:

  • Improves posture and spinal alignment
  • Calms the mind and reduces anxiety
  • Enhances concentration and focus
  • Stretches hips and increases flexibility
  • Reduces stress and muscle tension
  • Stimulates digestion and pelvic organs
  • Promotes emotional balance

Easy Pose (Sukhasana)

A comfortable seated position perfect for beginners, promoting relaxation and mindfulness without strain.

How to Perform:
  1. Sit cross-legged on a cushion or meditation mat
  2. Allow your knees to fall comfortably below your hips
  3. Keep your spine erect but relaxed, shoulders down
  1. Align your head directly over your pelvis
  2. Rest your hands on your knees or in your lap
  3. Breathe naturally and focus on the rise and fall of your abdomen
  4. Hold the position for 5-20 minutes, adjusting as needed

Benefits:

  • Reduces anxiety and mental fatigue
  • Promotes calmness and relaxation
  • Gentle on joints and accessible to all levels
  • Improves breathing and lung capacity
  • Strengthens back muscles
  • Creates a sense of grounding
  • Ideal for short daily meditation sessions

Corpse Pose (Savasana)

Complete relaxation pose for deep meditation and stress relief, allowing full surrender to the present moment.

How to Perform:
  1. Lie flat on your back on a comfortable surface
  2. Extend your arms at your sides, palms facing upward
  3. Allow your legs to fall open slightly, toes pointing outward
  1. Close your eyes and relax every muscle in your body
  2. Release tension from your forehead, jaw, shoulders, and hands
  3. Focus on natural breath without trying to control it
  4. Remain in the pose for 5-15 minutes, maintaining awareness

Benefits:

  • Reduces stress and anxiety significantly
  • Lowers blood pressure and heart rate
  • Improves sleep quality and combats insomnia
  • Relieves physical and mental fatigue
  • Calms the nervous system
  • Helps with headache and migraine relief
  • Promotes deep relaxation and mindfulness

Walking Meditation

Mindful movement practice that cultivates awareness through the simple act of walking with full presence.

How to Perform:
  1. Walk slowly in a quiet, peaceful space (about half normal speed)
  2. Sync your breath with your steps (inhale for 2-3 steps, exhale for 2-3 steps)
  3. Feel the contact between your feet and the ground with each step
  1. Maintain an upright but relaxed posture
  2. Stay present with physical sensations - the swing of your arms, the breeze on your skin
  3. When your mind wanders, gently return focus to your steps and breath
  4. Practice for 10-30 minutes, ideally in nature

Benefits:

  • Improves mindfulness and present-moment awareness
  • Provides gentle physical exercise
  • Reduces restlessness and anxiety
  • Deepens connection with nature and surroundings
  • Enhances circulation and digestion
  • Can be practiced anywhere, anytime
  • Combines movement with meditation benefits

Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana)

A calming forward fold that stretches the spine and hamstrings while promoting introspection.

How to Perform:
  1. Sit with legs extended straight in front of you
  2. Inhale and lengthen your spine, reaching arms overhead
  3. Exhale and fold forward from the hips, reaching for your feet
  1. Keep your spine long rather than rounding your back
  2. Relax your neck and shoulders
  3. Breathe deeply and hold for 1-3 minutes
  4. Release slowly on an inhale

Benefits:

  • Calms the brain and relieves stress
  • Stretches the spine and hamstrings
  • Stimulates the liver, kidneys and digestive organs
  • Improves posture and spinal alignment
  • Reduces anxiety and fatigue
  • Helps relieve symptoms of menopause
  • Therapeutic for high blood pressure

Half Lotus Pose (Ardha Padmasana)

A modified version of the full lotus pose that's easier on the knees while still providing stability for meditation.

How to Perform:
  1. Sit on the floor with legs extended straight in front of you
  2. Bend your right knee and place your right foot on your left thigh
  3. Keep your left leg extended or bend it under your right thigh
  1. Keep your spine straight and shoulders relaxed
  2. Place your hands on your knees in meditation mudra
  3. Switch leg positions after several minutes to balance the stretch
  4. Hold for 5-20 minutes, maintaining steady breathing

Benefits:

  • Easier alternative to full lotus pose
  • Stretches hips and knees gently
  • Improves posture and spinal alignment
  • Calms the mind and reduces stress
  • Increases flexibility in the legs
  • Prepares the body for full lotus pose
  • Promotes concentration and focus

Burmese Position

A comfortable seated position with both legs folded in front, ideal for those who find lotus poses challenging.

How to Perform:
  1. Sit on a cushion with both legs folded in front of you
  2. Place one ankle in front of the other (not stacked)
  3. Keep both knees resting comfortably on the floor
  1. Maintain a straight spine with shoulders relaxed
  2. Rest hands on knees or in your lap
  3. Close eyes and focus on natural breathing
  4. Hold for 10-30 minutes as comfortable

Benefits:

  • Gentle on knees and ankles
  • Promotes proper spinal alignment
  • Comfortable for extended meditation
  • Reduces lower back strain
  • Accessible to most body types
  • Encourages grounded feeling
  • Good introductory meditation pose

Seiza Pose

Traditional Japanese kneeling position that promotes alertness while maintaining comfort.

How to Perform:
  1. Kneel on the floor with knees together
  2. Sit back onto your heels with toes pointing backward
  3. Keep your spine straight and shoulders relaxed
  1. Place hands on thighs or in meditation mudra
  2. Use a cushion between thighs and calves if needed
  3. Breathe deeply and maintain the posture
  4. Hold for 5-15 minutes as comfortable

Benefits:

  • Improves digestion and posture
  • Strengthens thigh and back muscles
  • Promotes mental alertness
  • Stretches ankles and tops of feet
  • Good for those with tight hips
  • Encourages proper breathing
  • Traditional Zen meditation posture

Chair Meditation

An accessible meditation position using a chair, perfect for those with mobility limitations.

How to Perform:
  1. Sit on a straight-backed chair with feet flat on floor
  2. Keep knees at 90-degree angle, hip-width apart
  3. Rest hands on thighs or in lap
  1. Maintain natural curve in lower back
  2. Keep chin parallel to floor, crown lifting upward
  3. Close eyes and focus on breath
  4. Hold position for desired meditation duration

Benefits:

  • Accessible for all fitness levels
  • Reduces strain on joints
  • Good for office meditation
  • Maintains proper spinal alignment
  • Can be done anywhere with a chair
  • Ideal for seniors or injured practitioners
  • Equally effective as floor positions

Siddhasana (Adept's Pose)

An ancient meditation posture believed to channel energy upward through the spine.

How to Perform:
  1. Sit with legs extended, then bend left knee
  2. Place left heel against perineum
  3. Bend right knee and place right foot over left ankle
  1. Tuck right toes between left thigh and calf
  2. Keep spine straight, hands in chin or jnana mudra
  3. Maintain steady breathing pattern
  4. Hold for meditation duration, then switch sides

Benefits:

  • Stimulates energy flow through spine
  • Calms nervous system
  • Improves concentration
  • Strengthens pelvic floor muscles
  • Traditional pose for pranayama
  • Easier on knees than lotus
  • Believed to awaken kundalini energy

Vajrasana (Thunderbolt Pose)

A kneeling meditation pose that aids digestion and promotes mental clarity.

How to Perform:
  1. Kneel on floor with knees together
  2. Sit back onto heels with tops of feet flat on floor
  3. Keep spine erect and shoulders relaxed
  1. Place palms on thighs or in lap
  2. Close eyes and focus on breath
  3. Use cushion between thighs and calves if needed
  4. Hold for 5-20 minutes as comfortable

Benefits:

  • Aids digestion (ideal after meals)
  • Strengthens thigh muscles
  • Improves circulation to lower body
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Stretches ankles and feet
  • Promotes mental alertness
  • Good for sciatica relief

Standing Meditation

An active meditation posture that builds strength while cultivating mindfulness.

How to Perform:
  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
  2. Bend knees slightly, aligning over toes
  3. Keep spine straight, crown lifting upward
  1. Relax shoulders with arms at sides or in prayer position
  2. Engage core muscles gently
  3. Focus on breath and body sensations
  4. Hold for 5-15 minutes with soft gaze

Benefits:

  • Builds leg and core strength
  • Improves balance and posture
  • Energizes the body
  • Good for those who sit all day
  • Can be done anywhere
  • Connects with earth energy
  • Traditional qigong posture

Reclining Bound Angle Pose

A restorative meditation position that opens the heart and hips while promoting deep relaxation.

How to Perform:
  1. Lie on back with knees bent and feet flat
  2. Bring soles of feet together, letting knees fall outward
  3. Place arms at sides with palms facing up
  1. Use pillows under knees if needed for support
  2. Close eyes and focus on deep belly breathing
  3. Release all muscular tension
  4. Hold for 5-15 minutes

Benefits:

  • Relieves stress and fatigue
  • Opens hips and groin area
  • Stimulates abdominal organs
  • Improves circulation
  • Relieves menstrual discomfort
  • Good for varicose vein relief
  • Promotes deep relaxation

Lion's Pose (Simhasana)

An energizing meditation posture that releases tension through expressive breath and movement.

How to Perform:
  1. Kneel with hips resting on heels
  2. Place hands on knees, fingers spread wide
  3. Inhale deeply through nose
  1. Exhale forcefully while sticking out tongue and opening eyes wide
  2. Roar like a lion on the exhale
  3. Repeat 3-5 times then sit quietly
  4. Transition to silent meditation

Benefits:

  • Relieves facial tension
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Stimulates platysma muscle
  • Improves vocal clarity
  • Energizes the body
  • Good for throat chakra
  • Releases emotional blockages

Supported Child's Pose

A deeply restorative meditation variation that promotes surrender and introspection.

How to Perform:
  1. Kneel on floor with big toes touching
  2. Place bolster or stack of pillows lengthwise between thighs
  3. Fold forward over support with arms extended
  1. Turn head to one side, then switch halfway through
  2. Breathe deeply into back body
  3. Release all muscular effort
  4. Hold for 5-10 minutes

Benefits:

  • Relieves back and neck tension
  • Calms the nervous system
  • Encourages emotional release
  • Gently stretches hips and spine
  • Reduces fatigue and anxiety
  • Good for insomnia relief
  • Promotes deep introspection

Legs-Up-the-Wall Pose

A passive inversion that rejuvenates while allowing deep meditation.

How to Perform:
  1. Sit sideways with right hip against wall
  2. Swing legs up wall as you lie back
  3. Adjust hips close to wall (not touching if uncomfortable)
  1. Rest arms at sides with palms up
  2. Close eyes and focus on slow breathing
  3. Use folded blanket under hips if needed
  4. Hold for 5-15 minutes

Benefits:

  • Relieves tired legs and feet
  • Reduces mild backache
  • Calms the nervous system
  • Improves circulation
  • Helps reduce swelling in legs
  • Gentle inversion benefits
  • Promotes relaxation response

Crocodile Pose (Makarasana)

A prone meditation position that focuses awareness on the breath and abdominal movement.

How to Perform:
  1. Lie face down with legs extended
  2. Cross arms and rest forehead on stacked hands
  3. Separate heels slightly with toes pointing outward
  1. Close eyes and focus on belly pressing into floor on inhale
  2. Relax completely on each exhale
  3. Breathe deeply into back body
  4. Hold for 5-10 minutes

Benefits:

  • Improves diaphragmatic breathing
  • Relieves lower back tension
  • Calms the nervous system
  • Good for respiratory issues
  • Reduces fatigue and stress
  • Helps with digestive issues
  • Grounding and centering

Supported Bridge Pose

A gentle backbend meditation that opens the heart while maintaining relaxation.

How to Perform:

  2. Lift hips and slide yoga block or cushion under sacrum
  3. Extend legs straight or keep knees bent
  1. Rest arms at sides with palms up

Benefits:

  • Stretches chest and spine
  • Improves digestion
  • Reduces fatigue and anxiety
  • Helps relieve symptoms of menopause
  • Therapeutic for asthma and osteoporosis
  • Promotes emotional openness

Rocking Meditation

A rhythmic movement meditation that soothes the nervous system and quiets the mind.

How to Perform:
  1. Sit cross-legged on cushion or mat
  2. Place hands on knees or hold opposite elbows
  3. Begin gentle forward-backward rocking motion
  1. Let movement be natural and fluid
  2. Focus on sensation of movement
  3. Gradually slow rocking as mind quiets
  4. Transition to stillness when ready

Benefits:

  • Calms restless mind
  • Soothes nervous system
  • Improves body awareness
  • Releases spinal tension
  • Good for fidgety meditators
  • Helps transition from activity to stillness
  • Traditional Zen practice

Lying Spinal Twist

A reclining twist that releases spinal tension while maintaining meditative awareness.

How to Perform:
  1. Lie on back with arms extended in T position
  2. Bend right knee and draw it toward chest
  3. Guide knee across body to left side
  1. Keep both shoulders grounded
  2. Turn head to right if comfortable
  3. Breathe deeply into the twist
  4. Hold for 3-5 minutes per side

Benefits:

  • Relieves back and neck tension
  • Stimulates digestion
  • Improves spinal mobility
  • Calms the nervous system
  • Good for sciatica relief
  • Releases shoulder tension
  • Promotes relaxation

Mountain Pose (Tadasana) Meditation

A standing meditation that cultivates grounded presence and body awareness.

How to Perform:
  1. Stand with feet hip-width apart or together
  2. Distribute weight evenly through all four corners of feet
  3. Engage thigh muscles and lift kneecaps slightly
  1. Lengthen tailbone toward floor, crown toward ceiling
  2. Rest arms at sides or in prayer position
  3. Focus on breath and body alignment
  4. Hold for 5-15 minutes with soft gaze

Benefits:

  • Improves posture and balance
  • Strengthens legs and core
  • Increases body awareness
  • Reduces flat feet
  • Promotes grounded presence
  • Good for fatigue relief
  • Foundation for all standing poses
